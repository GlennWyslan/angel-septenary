Spiritual Preparation

Before beginning the 7-day Lent, it's essential to prepare yourself spiritually to receive the blessings of this sacred time of prayer.

Confession: If possible, take part in the sacrament of reconciliation prior to starting, so your heart is renewed.

Intention: Clearly define the special grace you wish to ask for during these 7 days. Write it down and keep it close to remind yourself.

Preparatory Fast: If your health allows, consider a light fast the day before you start (such as refraining from meat, sweets, or social media).

Prior Study: Spend a few quiet minutes learning more about the Priest's Prayer and his devotion to the guardian angel.

Suggested Prayer Times

Choosing the best moment for your daily prayers can greatly enhance your Lent journey. Here are some recommended times:

Morning (5–7am): The “Golden Hour” – In the early morning, the mind is calmer and more open. Priest’s Prayer often began his prayers as early as 4am.

Night (9–11pm): Before sleep, this is a peaceful moment for reflection and deeper communion through prayer.

Angel Hour (6pm): Traditionally the time to pray the Angelus, it’s a special window for angelic communication.

Important Tip: Try to keep the same time each day to help establish a consistent prayer habit. Set an alarm on your phone and activate “Do Not Disturb” during your sacred time.

Prayer Environment

The place where you pray greatly affects the depth of your connection. Create a space that inspires stillness and contemplation.

Dedicated Space: Set aside a small area in your home just for prayer. It doesn’t need to be large—just a space reserved for this sacred purpose.

Remove Distractions: Turn off the TV, computer, and phone. Let those around you know you’ll need a few quiet minutes.

Soft Lighting: Choose natural light or candles over harsh lighting. A dimly lit space helps create a reflective atmosphere.

Comfort with Awareness: Sit in a supportive chair or cushion, but avoid overly cozy positions that might make you sleepy.

Sacred Symbols: Include a crucifix, an image of the Guardian Angel’s Prayer, and your guardian angel in your prayer area.

Recommended Materials

To fully experience this 7-day journey, we suggest having the following nearby:

Holy Bible: For the daily readings throughout the 7-day Setenary.

White or Yellow Candle: Represents divine light and angelic presence. Light it during your prayers.

Spiritual Journal: To write down reflections, blessings, and personal insights during this time.

Rosary Beads: The Guardian Angel’s Prayer often called the rosary his “weapon.”

Image of the Guardian Angel’s Prayer: To help focus your thoughts and invite his intercession.

How to Pray the Devotions

To experience a deep and meaningful time of prayer, follow these guidelines:

Sign of the Cross: Always begin with the sign of the cross, calling on the Holy Trinity.

Call on the Holy Spirit: Ask for the Spirit’s guidance and enlightenment before starting.

Slow Reading: Read each prayer slowly, letting the words sink in and touch your heart.

Silent Pauses: Pause at key points to reflect and absorb what you just prayed.

Closing: Conclude with an Our Father, Hail Mary, and Glory Be, offering thanks to God, Our Lady, and the Guardian Angel’s Prayer.

Enhancing Your Spiritual Experience

To make the most of this 7-day Setenary journey, consider incorporating these extra practices:

Mass & Eucharist: If possible, attend daily Mass or at least on Sundays throughout the 7-day Setenary.

Acts of Kindness: Perform a daily act of charity, offering it with a specific intention.

Supplemental Reading: Read from the writings or stories related to the Guardian Angel’s Prayer to deepen your spiritual bond.

Prayer Partner: Connect with a friend or family member to share this journey and pray together.
